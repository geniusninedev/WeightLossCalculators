There are a few different kinds of fat in food. Controlled intake of some fat is good for health. Normally, saturated fat and trans fat are harmful. Monounsaturated fat, Polyunsaturated fat, and Omega-3 fatty acids are considered to be healthier. The recommended fat intake are listed below.

  • Take less than 10% of calories from saturated fats. Less than 7% to further reduce the risk to heart disease.

  • Replace solid fats with unsaturated fat if possible.

  • Take as little trans fat as possible.

  • Take less than 300 mg of dietary cholesterol per day.

Fat Intake Limit

Age 2 - 3

30% to 40% of Total Calories

Age 4 - 18

25% to 35% of Total Calories

Age 19+

20% to 35% of Total Calories
